ü§ù Cross-Functional Leadership Questions

"How do you translate business requirements into technical specifications for engineers?"
Your Process:
  1. Start with outcomes: What business result are we trying to achieve?
  2. Create user stories: How will different personas interact with this?
  3. Define measurables: How will we know it's successful?
  4. Prototype together: Low-fi wireframes that engineers can question and improve
  5. Iterative refinement: Technical constraints often reveal better solutions
Likely Follow-up: "Give me an example from your experience."
Your Example: "At Golden Learning Club, I needed to build AI conversations for seniors. I started with the outcome (reduce technology anxiety), created personas (tech-nervous vs. tech-curious), defined success (40%+ completion), prototyped with actual seniors, then worked with engineers to optimize for their specific interaction patterns."
"How do you handle disagreements between product, engineering, and design teams?"
Your EOS-Aligned Approach:
"I use a version of the IDS process: Identify the real constraint (usually time, tech debt, or user needs), Discuss with data and user research, Solve by focusing on the business outcome. Often disagreements happen when teams optimize for different metrics. Get everyone aligned on the primary measurable, and solutions become clearer."
"How do you ensure product decisions are clearly communicated across the organization?"
Your Communication Strategy:
"Three-tier communication: 1) Context - why this decision matters for OMG's mission, 2) Impact - how it affects each team/partner, 3) Action - what specific changes people can expect. I'd use OMG's existing partner channels and internal meetings, ensuring consistent messaging that connects product decisions to business outcomes."

üé≤ Challenging Scenarios & Problem-Solving

"A major partner says our AI-enhanced assessments feel 'too robotic' and their clients are complaining. How do you handle this?"
Your IDS Approach:
Identify: Is this a UX issue, a training issue, or a fundamental AI approach problem?
Discuss: Get specific feedback - what feels robotic? Compare completion rates and accuracy with/without AI features
Solve: Test solutions like more conversational AI prompts, better onboarding, or hybrid human/AI approaches while maintaining correlation standards
"You have limited engineering resources. Marketing wants a flashy new feature, but you know a backend improvement would have more impact. How do you decide?"
Your Decision Framework:
"I'd bring both teams together with data: what's the business impact of each option? Marketing features drive acquisition, but backend improvements drive retention and accuracy. I'd look for solutions that serve both needs - maybe the flashy feature can be built on the improved backend, or we can phase the work to deliver marketing value while building technical foundation."
"How would you handle pushback from the Product Science team about changing assessment questions they've carefully validated?"
Your Collaborative Approach:
"Their validation work is OMG's competitive advantage - I'd never override that lightly. Instead, I'd work with them to design experiments: can we A/B test new approaches while maintaining correlation standards? Can we enhance existing questions with AI without changing their core structure? The goal is evolution, not revolution."
Right People, Right Seats:
This shows you understand that Product Science team "gets it, wants it, has capacity" for validation work - your job is to enable their expertise, not replace it.
"What would you do if assessment correlation scores started declining after implementing new AI features?"
Your Crisis Response:
  1. Immediate action: Stop rollout and isolate the issue
  2. Root cause analysis: Is it the AI itself, the implementation, or external factors?
  3. Data segmentation: Which assessments/roles/industries are affected?
  4. Rapid iteration: Test fixes in controlled groups before full deployment
  5. Communication: Transparent updates to partners about the issue and resolution timeline
